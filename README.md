# UK Milk Imports and Exports Analysis

## Overview

This project analyzes the United Kingdom’s milk and cream trade using UN Comtrade data. The focus is on understanding the UK’s import–export balance, identifying key trading partners, and examining trade patterns during the first five months of 2015.

## Data Source

- United Nations Comtrade Database
- Commodities: Milk and cream (HS codes 0401, 0402)
- Period: January–May 2015
- File: `comtrade_milk_uk_jan_jul_15`

## Objectives

- Measure total milk and cream imports and exports
- Determine whether the UK has a trade surplus
- Identify major import and export partners
- Find regular monthly export customers
- Analyze countries with bi-directional trade

## Key Findings

- The UK recorded a trade surplus of over $100 million
- Ireland is the UK’s most significant trading partner
- The UK exported milk products to over 100 countries, but imported from only 21
- Regular customers account for over 75% of total exports
- Bi-directional trade exists with a limited number of countries
